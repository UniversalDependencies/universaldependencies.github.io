VERB: verb

Definition

A verb is a member of the syntactic class of words that typically signal events and actions, can constitute a minimal predicate in a clause, and govern the number and types of other constituents which may occur in the clause.

Despite its use in copular constructions, 是 / shì “be” is tagged as a verb due to its other non-copular meanings and functions.

Examples

  • 游泳 / yóuyǒng “swim”
  • 聽 / tīng “listen”
  • 呼吸 / hūxī “breathe”
  • 喜歡 / xǐhuān “like”
  • 決定 / juédìng “decide”
  • 有 / yǒu “have; own; exist”
  • 是 / shì “be; exist”
