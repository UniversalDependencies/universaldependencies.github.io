Gender: gender

Gender

The possible values for Gender in Pomak is masculine, feminine and neuter. It occurs with nominal parts of speech.

Masc: masculine gender

Nouns denoting male persons are masculine. Other nouns may be also grammatically masculine, without any relation to sex.

Examples [qpm]:

Nouns:

  • góram “[a] tree”
  • pot “[a] street, [a] road”
  • sólmen “[a] snail”
  • mantár “[a] mashroom”
  • hlæb “[a] bread”
  • kóren “[a] root”

Adjectives:

  • koráv “hard”
  • suróv “raw / fresh”
  • prav “right”
  • zelén “green”
  • visók “tall”
  • star “old”

Passive Participles:

  • umarén “tired”
  • adbáven “spoiled / rotten”
  • izgorǽt “burnt”
  • písan “written”
  • kúpen “bought”
  • amýt “washed”

Fem: feminine gender

Nouns denoting female persons are feminine. Other nouns may be also grammatically feminine, without any relation to sex.

Examples [qpm]:

Nouns:

  • glavá “[a]head”
  • maglá “[a] fog”
  • godína “[a] year”
  • zemǽ “[a] ground”
  • žaná “[a] woman”
  • kráva “[a] cow”

Adjectives:

  • koráva “hard”
  • suróva “raw / fresh”
  • pravá “right”
  • zeléna “green”
  • visóka “tall”
  • stará “old”

Passive Participles:

  • umaréna “tired”
  • adbávena “spoiled / rotten”
  • izgorǽta “burnt”
  • písana “written”
  • kúpena “bought”
  • amýta “washed”

Neut: neuter gender

Neither masculine nor feminine (grammatically).

Examples [qpm]:

Simple Nouns:

  • déte “[a] chld”
  • kóte “[a] cat”
  • sélo “[a] village”
  • séme “[a] seed”
  • lísto “[a] leaf”
  • krílo “[a] wing”

Verbal Nouns:

  • dúmeņe “[a] speech”
  • rábateņe “[a] work”
  • sedéņe “sitting around”
  • vráštaņe “[a] return”
  • znáņe “knowledge”
  • spaņé “sleep”

Adjectives:

  • korávo “hard”
  • suróvo “spoiled / rotten”
  • právo “right”
  • zeléno “green”
  • visóko “tall”
  • stáro “old”

Passive Participles:

  • umaréno “tired”
  • adbáveno “spoilt / rotten”
  • izgorǽto ‘burnt”
  • písano “written”
  • kúpeno “bought”
  • amýto “washed”
