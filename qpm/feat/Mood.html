Mood: mood

Mood

Mood is a feature that expresses modality and subclassifies finite verb forms. In Pomak there are two moods: Indicative and Imperative.

Ind: indicative

Indicative can be considered the default mood. A verb in the indicative mood merely states that something happens, has happened or will happen, without adding any attitude of the speaker.

Examples

  • Isén rábati faf bángono “Isen works at the bank”
  • Vremǿso so óblači i móža da zaletí “The weather becomes cloudy and it might rain”
  • Rábatasa ní kugá na svóršava “Work never ends [lit: the work never ends]”
  • Hódil je mlóziš na Amériko “He has been many times to America”
  • Mlózi insán vazgeštísaho tütǘnese “Many people have abandoned the tobacco [cultivation]”

Imp: imperative

The speaker uses imperative to order or ask the addressee to do the action denoted by the verb. Pomak distinguishes two types of imperative at the morphological level: imperative mood and prohibitive mood. Imperative is simpler and easier to use than the prohibitive, which is formed by means of the particles na / ne (negative) and namój / namójte (prohibitive). The particle na / ne precedes prohibitive verb forms while the particle namój / namójte precedes infinitives.

Examples

  • Imperative:

    • Zǿmijte sí sas vámi šimšijó - móža da zaletí “Take the umbrella with you - it might rain”
    • Daj mí annók bardáka vódo, akú tí je ne zahmét “Give me a glass of water, if it is not difficult for you”
    • Valésavyj kadé hódiš i kaná práviš :Be careful where you go and what you do”
    • Pýtyj mó kanáta íšteš “Ask me whatever you want”
    • Glǿdyj kutrí dúmi! “Look who is talking”

  • Prohibitive:

    • Whenna / neprecedes prohibitive verb forms:
      • Na dumíj huzlý, še razbudíš déteso “Do not speak loud, you will wake up the child”
      • Na izlizáj na gulǽma póte samýj “Do not go to a long journey alone”
      • Na baráj čúzdy rábaty “Do not touch other people’s things”
      • Zǿmij isazí i na pytáj ní kaná “Take this and do not ask anything”
      • Akú tí dávot nǽko, na zimáj go “If they give you something, do not take it”
    • Whennamój / namójte precedes infinitives:
      • Namójte kazavá ní kutrómu “Do not say [it] to anyone”
      • Namój nagadá mlógo játo, da na astáne “Do not cook a lot of food, so as not be left over”
      • Namój slušá kaná tí kázavot drúzine “Do not listen to what others tell you”
      • Namójte barzá, ráno sí je “Do not hurry, it is still early”
      • Namój zabaravǽ kutrí tí pamógna da atvóriš dükǽnate “Do not forget, who helped you open the shop”
