acl: clausal modifier of noun

acl is used for finite and non-finite clauses that modify a noun.

In the Scottish Gaelic treebank relative clauses have the relation acl:relcl.

acl is used specifically for nouns that represent some sort of psychological state: knowing, hoping and so on. They are usually attached non-projectively.
