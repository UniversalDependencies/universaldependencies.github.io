VerbClass: verb class

Values: 2lit 3lit 4lit 5lit 2aeinf 3aeinf 5aeinf 2aered 3aered Caus2lit Caus3lit Caus3aeinf Caus2aered

There are verbs with 1 to 6 radicals (Schenkel 2012: 183). If all radicals are strong, the abbreviation lit (literal) is used, for example č̣ṭ “say” is a 2-lit. verb. If the last radical is weak, the abbreviation inf (infirma i.e. weak) is used, for example mri̯ “love” is a 3ae-inf verb. Verbs with a reduplicated radical are labelled with the abbreviation red (reduplicata) and causative verbs with the abbreviation caus.

2lit: verb with 2 radicals, both strong

Examples

  • č̣ṭ “say”

3aeinf: verb with 3 radicals, the last one weak

Examples

  • mri̯ “love”
