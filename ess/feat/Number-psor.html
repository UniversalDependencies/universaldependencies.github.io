Number[psor]: possessor’s number

The possessor’s number is marked morphologically on the possessed noun (which also has its own morphological number inflection).

Sing: singular possessor

Examples

  • [ess] qikmiqa “my dog” Number=Sing|Number[psor]=Sing
  • [ess] qikmigka “my two dogs” Number=Dual|Number[psor]=Sing
  • [ess] qikminka “my dogs” Number=Plur|Number[psor]=Sing

Dual: dual possessor

Examples

  • [ess] qikmighpung “our (dual) dog” Number=Sing|Number[psor]=Dual
  • [ess] qikmigpung “our (dual) two dogs” Number=Dual|Number[psor]=Dual
  • [ess] qikmipung “our (dual) dogs” Number=Plur|Number[psor]=Dual

Plur: plural possessor

Examples

  • [ess] qikmighput “our (plural) dog” Number=Sing|Number[psor]=Plur
  • [ess] qikmigput “our (plural) two dogs” Number=Dual|Number[psor]=Plur
  • [ess] qikmiput “our (plural) dogs” Number=Plur|Number[psor]=Plur
